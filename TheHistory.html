The History of the Patriots

In the American Football League (AFL), the Patriots were formerly known as the Boston Patriots. Despite being one of the eight initial AFL teams, they had a difficult start to the season. They relocated to Foxborough and changed their name to the New England Patriots in 1971. Success was scarce at the time, and the team didn't make it to the Super Bowl until 1985. Even though they were defeated by the Chicago Bears, it was a big step in making the squad famous.

When they hired Bill Belichick as head coach in 2000 and selected Tom Brady the following year, it was a watershed in their history. Between 2001 and 2018, this squad won six Super Bowls under their leadership. With a total of nine Super Bowl appearances under Belichick and Brady, the Patriots rose to prominence throughout this time. They set new benchmarks for what it meant to be a successful NFL team and broke records.

Their accomplishment wasn't without controversy, though. Incidents like "Spygate" and "Deflategate" brought the squad criticism, but it didn't stop them for long. Regardless of whether you consider the Patriots to be heroes or villains, their history is an intriguing tale of tenacity, creativity, and power.
